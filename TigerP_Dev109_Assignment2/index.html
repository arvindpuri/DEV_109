Seafood Paella Recipe

Hi, This is Tiger.

Like many of you, I like seafood paella.

During the SARS-COV2 Pandemic, when many restaurants shut down, I learned to cook my favorite dishes at home. Seafood Paella is one of my favorite Spanish food recipes.

Seafood paella is a vibrant and flavorful dish that originates from Valencia, Spain. This recipe combines a variety of fresh seafood with rice, saffron, and vegetables, creating a stunning centerpiece for any meal.

Ingredients

Type List
Seafood
  • 12 raw jumbo shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • fresh mussels, cleaned
  • 1 tube of squid, cleaned and cut into small squares
  • Optional: lobster tails or clams for added flavor
Vegetables
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 onion, finely diced
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes (fresh or canned
Rice and Broth
  • 1 cup uncooked short-grain rice (such as Bomba or Arborio)
  • 2.5 cups fish broth (or shrimp stock)
Seasoning
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprikas
  • Sea salt and black pepper to tastes
  • Olive oil for cookings
Garnish
  • Lemon wedges
  • Finely chopped parsley

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    2. Roughly chop the garlic, dice the onion, and chop the bell pepper. Cut the squid into small squares.
  2. Cook the Seafood:
    3. In a large paella pan or deep skillet, heat about 1/3 cup of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the squid and cook for about 1.5 to 2 minutes until slightly golden. Remove from the pan and set aside. In the same pan, add the diced onion and garlic; saut√© until translucent (about 3 minutes). Then add the bell pepper and cook for an additional minute.
  3. Add Tomatoes and Spices:
    4. Stir in the diced tomatoes, smoked paprika, sea salt, and black pepper. Cook for about 2 minutes until the mixture thickens.
  4. Incorporate Rice and Broth:
    5. Pour in the fish broth and add the saffron. Bring to a boil. Once boiling, add the rice, stirring to ensure it is evenly distributed. Do not stir again after this point.
  5. Add Seafood:
    6. After about 5 minutes of cooking, add the shrimp and mussels to the pan. Reduce heat to low-medium and let simmer until all liquid is absorbed (approximately another 10-15 minutes).
  6. Create Socarrat:
    7. To achieve socarrat (the crispy bottom layer), increase the heat to medium-high for an additional minute or two at the end of cooking.
  7. Serve:
    8. Remove from heat and cover with a clean kitchen towel for about 5 minutes. Garnish with lemon wedges and chopped parsley before serving.

This seafood paella is not only a feast for the eyes but also a delightful blend of flavors that brings a taste of Spain to your dining table. Enjoy!
